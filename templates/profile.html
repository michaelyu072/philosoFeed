Marcus Aurelius

Stoicism • 121 AD - 180 AD

Roman Emperor and a defining figure of Stoic philosophy. Best known for his private notes to himself, now known as the "Meditations".

history_edu

Biography

Marcus Aurelius was Roman emperor from 161 to 180 and a Stoic philosopher. He was the last of the rulers known as the Five Good Emperors, and the last emperor of the Pax Romana, an age of relative peace and stability for the Roman Empire. He served as Roman consul in 140, 145, and 161.

His "Meditations," written in Greek while on campaign between 170 and 180, is still revered as a literary monument to a philosophy of service and duty, describing how to find and preserve equanimity in the midst of conflict by following nature as a source of guidance and inspiration.

Philosophy in a Nutshell

"The happiness of your life depends upon the quality of your thoughts."

Core Belief: Stoicism teaches the development of self-control and fortitude as a means of overcoming destructive emotions. It emphasizes that virtue is the only good and that we should live in harmony with reason and nature.
